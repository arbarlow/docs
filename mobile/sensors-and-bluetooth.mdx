## Bluetooth Sensor Support

The Stride app connects to standard Bluetooth and ANT+ (with adapter) sensors for comprehensive training data.

## Initial Setup

### Bluetooth Requirements

**Phone Settings**:
- Bluetooth enabled
- Location services on (required for Bluetooth)
- Stride app has Bluetooth permission
- Phone not in airplane mode

**Sensor Preparation**:
- Fresh batteries installed
- Sensors powered on
- Within range (10 feet)
- Not connected to other apps

## Connecting Sensors

### First Time Connection

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Sensor Settings">
    In Stride app: **Settings → Sensors**
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Sensor Type">
    Choose what you're connecting:
    - Power Meter
    - Heart Rate
    - Speed/Cadence
    - Smart Trainer
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Activate Sensor">
    - **Power meter**: Spin cranks
    - **HR strap**: Wet and wear
    - **Speed/Cadence**: Spin wheel/cranks
    - **Trainer**: Power on
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select from List">
    Tap your sensor when it appears
    - Shows signal strength
    - Displays sensor ID
    - May show battery level
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm Connection">
    - Green checkmark appears
    - Test data displayed
    - Save sensor profile
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Auto-Connect

Once paired, sensors auto-connect when:
- Starting a new activity
- Sensors are active
- Bluetooth is enabled
- Within range

## Power Meter Setup

### Types Supported

**Direct Power Measurement**:
- Pedal-based (Favero, Garmin, PowerTap)
- Crank-based (Stages, 4iiii, Quarq)
- Spider-based (Power2Max, SRM)
- Hub-based (PowerTap)

**Smart Trainer Power**:
- Wahoo KICKR series
- Tacx NEO series
- Elite trainers
- Saris trainers

### Calibration

<Warning>
  Always calibrate your power meter before important rides or tests for accurate data.
</Warning>

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Zero Offset">
    **Most Common Calibration**
    1. Ensure bike is stationary
    2. Unclip from pedals
    3. In Stride: **Sensors → Power → Calibrate**
    4. Follow on-screen prompts
    5. Wait for confirmation
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Spindown">
    **For Trainers**
    1. Warm up trainer 10 minutes
    2. Reach specified speed
    3. Stop pedaling
    4. Let wheel spin down
    5. Calibration calculated
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Manual Entry">
    **For Some Meters**
    1. Check manufacturer app
    2. Note calibration value
    3. Enter in Stride settings
    4. Update when changes
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Dual-Sided Power

For dual-sided meters:
- Shows left/right balance
- Tracks pedaling efficiency
- Identifies imbalances
- Helps with bike fit

## Heart Rate Monitors

### Connection Best Practices

**Chest Straps** (Recommended):
1. Wet electrode pads
2. Position below chest muscles
3. Adjust strap snugness
4. Check for stable reading

**Optical Sensors**:
- Ensure tight fit
- Position correctly on wrist/arm
- Clean sensor window
- Note: Less accurate at high intensities

### Troubleshooting HR

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Erratic Readings">
    - Re-wet chest strap
    - Check battery level
    - Move away from interference
    - Try different position
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="No Signal">
    - Verify strap is on correctly
    - Check if already paired elsewhere
    - Replace battery
    - Try manual search
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Drops Out">
    - Tighten strap
    - Check for worn electrodes
    - Update sensor firmware
    - Consider new strap
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Speed and Cadence

### Sensor Placement

**Speed Sensors**:
- Hub-mounted (most accurate)
- Fork-mounted (easy access)
- GPS backup available

**Cadence Sensors**:
- Crank arm (standard)
- Shoe-based (some systems)
- Pedal-integrated

### Wheel Circumference

For accurate speed:
1. Measure wheel circumference
2. Enter in **Settings → Bike Profile**
3. Different for each bike
4. Accounts for tire size

**Quick Reference** (700c):
- 23mm tire: 2096mm
- 25mm tire: 2111mm  
- 28mm tire: 2136mm
- 32mm tire: 2169mm

## Smart Trainer Control

### Connecting for ERG Mode

<Steps>
  <Step title="Trainer Preparation">
    - Power on trainer
    - Ensure firmware updated
    - Not paired to other apps
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Multiple Connections">
    Smart trainers provide:
    - Power data
    - Controllable resistance
    - Cadence (usually)
    - Speed (simulated)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select All Functions">
    When connecting, enable:
    - ✅ Power Source
    - ✅ Controllable Trainer
    - ✅ Cadence Source
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Test Control">
    - Start free ride
    - Adjust target power
    - Feel resistance change
    - Confirm ERG working
  </Step>
</Steps>

### ERG Mode Tips

**For Best Experience**:
- Maintain steady cadence (85-95 rpm)
- Don't shift gears during ERG
- Allow 5-10 seconds for adjustment
- Disable for sprints

## Battery Management

### Expected Battery Life

| Sensor Type | Battery Type | Expected Life |
|------------|--------------|---------------|
| Power Meter | CR2032 | 100-300 hours |
| HR Strap | CR2032 | 300-500 hours |
| Speed/Cadence | CR2032 | 12-18 months |
| Trainer | AC Power | N/A |

### Low Battery Indicators

- ⚠️ Warning in app
- Erratic readings
- Connection issues
- Reduced range

<Info>
  **Pro Tip**: Keep spare CR2032 batteries in your bike bag. Change batteries before important events.
</Info>

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can't Find Sensor">
    1. Toggle Bluetooth off/on
    2. Force quit Stride app
    3. Restart phone
    4. Check sensor battery
    5. Reset sensor (see manual)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Keeps Disconnecting">
    - Move phone closer
    - Check for interference
    - Update app/firmware
    - Replace battery
    - Unpair and re-pair
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Wrong Data">
    - Calibrate sensors
    - Check settings (wheel size, etc.)
    - Verify correct sensor selected
    - Look for damage
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Interference">
    Sources to avoid:
    - Other Bluetooth devices
    - WiFi routers
    - Microwaves
    - Some LED lights
    - Other riders' sensors
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Factory Reset

When all else fails:
1. Remove sensor from app
2. Reset sensor (check manual)
3. Replace battery
4. Re-pair as new device

## Group Ride Considerations

### In Large Groups

- More interference possible
- Consider ANT+ over Bluetooth
- Pair before meeting
- Have backup recording

### Sensor Sharing

Some sensors allow multiple connections:
- Check manufacturer specs
- Usually 2-3 devices max
- May affect battery life
- Can cause conflicts
